MARS

The Red Planet

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun in our Solar System. Named after the Roman god of war, this planet has been nicknamed the "Red Planet." The first observations of Mars were recorded as far back as the era of the ancient Egyption astronomers. However, detailed measurements and studies was not recorded until Babylonian astronomers developed arithmetic to predict future positions of the planet. Until the invention of the telescope in the 1600s, observers only could observe that which can be seen by the naked eye. More detailed observations were possible with the first telescope observation of Mars by Galileo Galilei in 1610. Quickly thereafter, scientists discovered distinct features of the planet to uncover its history and further understand the physics, chemistry, and geology of the planet.

This terrestrial planet is identified by its red color due to the high levels of iron oxide on the surface. Mars is a close neighbor of Earth and approximately half the diameter of Earth and about 11% of Earth's mass. Given this relatively small diameter, it would take just over six planets that are the size of Mars to fill the volume of the Earth. The tilt of its axis also gives a relatively similar day length on Mars that we can relate to on Earth. The internal structure of Mars is said to consist of a crust, mantle, and core. However, there is not enough evidence to conclude whether or not there is a layered core of the planet like Earth's liquid outer core and solid inner core.

Do not be deceived by the barren, desolate appearance of Mars. There are many exciting features on the surface that give evidence for tectonics, volcanism, and water if we look a little closer. These features give the planet an enticing reason to consider inhabiting Mars and companies are looking to the future and beginning thought of how to make living there possible. Explore further to learn about the mystery of Mars' past and discover what geologic features give important clues on the past, present, and future potential of the Red Planet.
